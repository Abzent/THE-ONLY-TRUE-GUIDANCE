Fasting on Ramadan

Sharii definition: To worship Allah by abstaining from eating, drinking, and other matters that void your fast from pre-dawn to sunset (with correct intention).

Fasting was decreed to gain Taqwa / Taqwa means doing that which Allah has enjoined and avoiding that which He has forbidden.

Quran: O you who believe! Observing As-Saum (the fasting) is prescribed for you as it was prescribed for those before you, that you may become Al-Muttaqûn (Al-Baqarah 2:183).

Quran: Verily, Allah accepts only from those who are Al-Muttaqûn (the pious).(Al-Mâidah : 27)

Fasting on Ramadan is a wajib and a pillar of Islam.

Quran: Allah (SWT) said “So whoever of you is present at his home, he must observe the fasting of that month”(Al-Baqarah 2:185).

Hadith: reported by Bukhari and Muslim in which the Messenger (ﷺ) states: It is narrated on the authority of ('Abdullah) son of Umar (رضي الله عنه) that the Prophet (ﷺ) said: (The superstructure of) al-Islam is raised on five (pillars), i. e. the oneness of Allah, the establishment of prayer, payment of Zakat, the, fast of Ramadan, Pilgrimage (to Mecca). Sahih Muslim 16 a

The Niyyah (Intention)

It is a condition of the fasting person to have the correct intention to fast. The intention is not to be uttered on the tongue; rather it is in the heart (your desire to fast).

Hadith: Yahya related to me from Malik from Nafi that Abdullah ibn Umar used to say, “Only some one who makes the intention to fast before dawn (actually) fasts.” Muwatta Malik Book 18, Hadith 5
